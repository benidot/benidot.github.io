genres

› post-rock

subgenre of experimental rock that emphasizes texture, atmosphere, and non-traditional song structures over conventional rock techniques

› ambient

genre of music that emphasizes tone and atmosphere over traditional musical structure or rhythm • ambient music uses textural layers of sound that can reward both passive and active listening

› metalcore

subgenre of heavy metal with slow intense breakdowns, screaming vocals and heavy riffs, as well as double bass drumming

› art rock

subgenre of rock music that generally reflects a challenging or avant-garde approach to rock, or which makes use of modernist, experimental, or unconventional elements

› vaporwave

defined partly by slowed-down, chopped and screwed samples of smooth jazz, 1970s elevator music, r&b, and lounge music

› chamber pop

genre that combines rock music with the intricate use of strings, horns, piano, and vocal harmonies, and other components drawn from the orchestral and lounge pop of the 1960s, with an emphasis on melody and texture

› noise rock

noise-oriented style of experimental rock that spun off from punk rock in the 1980s • drawing on movements such as minimalism, artists indulge in extreme distortion through electric guitars and electronic instrumentation

› ethereal folk

dreamy, underwater, floaty subgenre of indie folk

› chamber folk

style of typically gentle fingerpicked acoustic guitar or harp-led music

› microtonal harp / experimental violin

what i wanna call leya next to chamber folk, alternate tunings on harp and violin that make harsh soundscapes

› folktronica

genre of indie electronic music comprising elements of folk music and electronica, often using stringed instruments & delicate, textural sound design

› electronica

broad group of electronic-based music styles intended for active listening rather than strictly for dancing

› post-hardcore

punk rock music genre that maintains the aggression and intensity of hardcore punk but emphasizes a greater degree of creative expression
